# Paper Cutout Design System

Layered, three-dimensional collage style composed of overlapping flat sheets of textured paper with clean cut edges and realistic drop shadows.

---

## 1. Visual Theme & Atmosphere

**Brand Personality:**
- Craft-centered, organic, and friendly
- Tactile, playful, and dimensional
- Creative, artisanal, and layered

**Key Characteristics:**
- Overlapping layers of flat cardstock paper
- Clean, crisp, scissor-cut or laser-cut edges on all shapes
- Distinct physical drop shadows cast from upper layers onto lower layers
- Subtle, organic craft paper or watercolor paper textures
- Stylized silhouettes and abstract geometric forms

---

## 2. Color Palette & Roles

**Primary Colors:**
- Natural, harmonious colors that feel like dyed cardstock or construction paper.

| Role | Color | Hex | Usage |
|------|-------|-----|-------|
| Deepest Shadow | Dark warm gray | `#2E2A27` | Inner shadows, base layers |
| Paper White | Unbleached white | `#F9F6F0` | Bright highlights, top-level cards |

**Accent Colored Cardstock:**
| Role | Color | Hex | Usage |
|------|-------|-----|-------|
| Mustard Yellow | Ochre yellow | `#E5A93C` | Warm foreground shapes, active states |
| Forest Green | Sage green | `#4A7055` | Secondary background layers |
| Terracotta | Burnt sienna | `#C35B48` | Primary visual accents |

---

## 3. Typography Rules

- Shapes act as containers.
- Simple, friendly sans-serif or clean geometric fonts.

---

## 4. Do's and Don't's

### Do's
- Create a clear sense of depth with multi-layered sheets of paper
- Use soft, realistic drop shadows between paper layers (no flat outlines)
- Ensure all edges look clean-cut and sharp
- Maintain a subtle physical paper texture on all surfaces

### Don't
- Don't use smooth digital gradients or airbrushed lighting
- Don't use flat outlines (use shadows to differentiate overlapping layers)
- Don't make the layers look transparent or glossy
