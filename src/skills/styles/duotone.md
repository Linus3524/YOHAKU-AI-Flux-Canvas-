# Duotone Design System

High-contrast, dual-color graphic treatment mapping shadows and highlights to two highly contrasting primary colors, inspired by Spotify poster art.

---

## 1. Visual Theme & Atmosphere

**Brand Personality:**
- Energetic, punchy, and contemporary
- High contrast, editorial, and stylized
- Minimalist yet loud

**Key Characteristics:**
- All visual depth is mapped strictly onto a two-color gradient map
- Deep shadows are converted to a single rich dark color (e.g., deep blue)
- Bright highlights are converted to a single vibrant light color (e.g., hot pink)
- Midtones are a smooth blend between the two selected hues
- Flat graphic compositions or stylized portraits with absolute color uniformity

---

## 2. Color Palette & Roles

**Primary Duotone Pair (Spotify Classic style):**
| Role | Color | Hex | Usage |
|------|-------|-----|-------|
| Shadow Hue | Deep electric indigo | `#0A0A8E` | All shadows, dark areas, text borders |
| Highlight Hue | Hot neon magenta | `#FF2D9B` | All highlights, background glow, active buttons |

---

## 3. Typography Rules

- Extremely bold, heavy-weight typography (e.g., "Helvetica Neue Heavy", "Futura Bold").
- Text should contrast strongly by using the highlight hue over shadow background, or vice versa.

---

## 4. Do's and Don'ts

### Do's
- Restrict the entire image to precisely two primary hues (shadow color + highlight color)
- Apply high-contrast mapping so details are sharp and graphic
- Keep backgrounds integrated into the duotone map

### Don'ts
- Don't introduce third colors (like white highlights or yellow borders)
- Don't use standard realistic shading or multi-colored gradients
- Don't use low-contrast color pairs (e.g., dark blue and dark green)

---

### Example Component Prompts
