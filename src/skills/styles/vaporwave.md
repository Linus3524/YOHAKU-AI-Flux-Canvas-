# Vaporwave Design System

Nostalgic, dreamy digital art style blending 1980s-90s consumer aesthetics, early 3D computer graphics, and lo-fi retro-futurism.

---

## 1. Visual Theme & Atmosphere

**Brand Personality:**
- Dreamy, melancholic, and nostalgic
- Surreal, retro-tech, and consumerist-ironic
- Glitchy, low-fidelity, and ambient

**Key Characteristics:**
- Glowing sunset gradients and pastel grids stretching into the horizon
- Classic marble busts, ionic pillars, and architectural wireframes
- Windows 95 UI dialogs, retro computer mouse cursors, and early internet icons
- Palm trees, tropical beaches, and low-res pixel graphics
- VHS scan lines, chromatic aberration, and neon color blooms

---

## 2. Color Palette & Roles

**Primary Colors:**
| Role | Color | Hex | Usage |
|------|-------|-----|-------|
| Synth Purple | Violet | `#8B5CF6` | Background grids, ambient shadows |
| Magenta Glow | Bright fuchsia | `#EC4899` | Sunset gradients, primary accents |
| Ocean Cyan | Bright turquoise | `#06B6D4` | Highlighting, secondary accents, wireframes |
| Pastel Pink | Blush pink | `#FBCFE8` | Card backgrounds, paper overlays |

---

## 3. Typography Rules

**Font Family:**
- Primary: Retro sans-serif or early digital display fonts
- System UI: Classic serif or pixel fonts (e.g., "Times New Roman", "MS Sans Serif")

---

## 4. Do's and Don'ts

### Do's
- Use glowing pastel pink-to-purple and cyan gradients
- Use early computer UI elements (like grey window title bars, bevels)
- Combine classic Greek/Roman sculpture elements with digital grids
- Apply lo-fi VHS textures or neon glow blooms

### Don'ts
- Don't use modern flat material color palettes (like Slate Gray or pure corporate Navy)
- Don't make layouts look too clean, sterile, or professional
- Don't use high-contrast modern shadows without neon color cast
